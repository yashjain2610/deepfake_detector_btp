A Compression-Resilient Deepfake Detector via Attentive Spatial–Frequency Fusion

Yash Aggarwal, Yash Jain
Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Delhi Technological University, Delhi, India

ABSTRACT
Deepfake detection models often fail to generalize across datasets and degrade sharply under lossy compression, limiting real-world deployment. We present a dual-stream deepfake detector that fuses complementary spatial and frequency-domain cues using a channel-wise gated attention mechanism. The spatial stream uses an ImageNet-pretrained Xception backbone to capture macro inconsistencies, while the frequency stream analyzes per-channel Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) maps with a lightweight ResNet-18 backbone. A learned 512-dimensional gate adaptively blends the two embeddings, enabling robustness when frequency cues are weakened by compression. We evaluate in-domain performance on an internal FaceForensics++ validation split and cross-dataset generalization by training on FaceForensics++ and testing on Celeb-DF-v2. To avoid misleading results from fixed thresholds, we select operating thresholds on the validation split and report threshold-free metrics (AUROC, AP) as well as operating-point metrics (EER, TPR@FPR, balanced accuracy). Our model achieves AUROC of 0.9955 in-domain (video-level) and 0.9018 cross-dataset on Celeb-DF-v2 (video-level), demonstrating competitive generalization under a realistic evaluation protocol.

KEYWORDS
Deepfake detection; face forgery; frequency domain; DCT; generalization; compression robustness; AUROC; operating point evaluation.

1. INTRODUCTION
Deepfake detectors frequently suffer a “domain gap”: strong in-domain accuracy but substantial failure under distribution shift. This is often due to shortcut learning—overfitting to dataset-specific artifacts rather than generator-agnostic manipulation traces. In real deployments, lossy compression further removes high-frequency forensic cues. A deployable detector must integrate complementary cues and be evaluated using threshold-free and operating-point metrics rather than a fixed probability threshold.

Contributions:
1) Dual-stream detector combining an RGB spatial stream (Xception) and a frequency stream (per-channel DCT maps) fused via a channel-wise gate.
2) Compression-aware training augmentations (JPEG simulation) and frequency masking to reduce brittle shortcut reliance.
3) Paper-ready evaluation protocol: thresholds selected on FF++ validation and applied to cross-dataset testing; report AUROC/AP plus EER, TPR@FPR, and balanced accuracy.

2. RELATED WORK
Datasets: FaceForensics++ and Celeb-DF are widely used benchmarks. Cross-dataset testing (FF++→Celeb-DF-v2) is substantially harder than in-domain testing.
Backbones: Xception is a strong CNN baseline frequently used for deepfake detection.
Frequency cues: High-frequency residuals and frequency-domain features can improve generalization but can be sensitive to compression; hybrid fusion is common. We focus on adaptive channel-wise fusion for stability under compression.

3. METHOD
Overview: two parallel streams process an input face crop I (HxWx3).
Spatial stream: ImageNet-pretrained Xception produces a 2048-D embedding.
Frequency stream: compute per-channel DCT log-magnitude maps and process with ResNet-18 to produce a 512-D embedding.
Both embeddings are projected to 512-D and fused with a learned gate g in (0,1)^512:
  z = g ⊙ z_s + (1−g) ⊙ z_f
Classification head: small MLP outputs one logit (Real vs Fake).

4. EXPERIMENTAL SETUP
Preprocessing: faces detected/cropped and resized to 299×299.
Protocol:
  - In-domain: internal FF++ validation split (frames grouped by video folder).
  - Cross-dataset: train on FF++ and test on Celeb-DF-v2.
Video-level aggregation: mean of per-frame probabilities per video folder.
Operating points: thresholds selected on FF++ validation (Youden’s J) and applied everywhere. Report AUROC/AP (threshold-free) plus EER, TPR@FPR, and balanced accuracy.

5. RESULTS
Thresholds selected on FF++ validation and applied to Celeb-DF-v2.

Main results (Youden threshold):
  - FF++ internal validation:
      Frame: AUROC 0.9804, AP 0.9969, EER 0.0690, BalAcc 0.9345
      Video: AUROC 0.9955, AP 0.9990, EER 0.0313, BalAcc 0.9751
  - Celeb-DF-v2 (FF++→CDFv2):
      Frame: AUROC 0.8227, AP 0.9752, EER 0.2596, BalAcc 0.7372
      Video: AUROC 0.9018, AP 0.9873, EER 0.1749, BalAcc 0.8093

Discussion: Video aggregation improves performance. Cross-dataset AUROC 0.90 is competitive but below recent higher-capacity multi-module approaches. Low-FPR operating points reduce TPR by construction; therefore balanced accuracy and Youden-selected thresholds provide a more realistic “balanced” deployment setting than a fixed 0.5 threshold on an imbalanced test set.

6. CONCLUSION
We presented a dual-stream detector with channel-wise gated fusion of spatial RGB and DCT-frequency cues. Under a validation-selected threshold protocol, it achieves strong in-domain performance and competitive cross-dataset generalization to Celeb-DF-v2. Future work: evaluate on DFDC, add robustness plots under controlled compression, and explore temporal modeling.
